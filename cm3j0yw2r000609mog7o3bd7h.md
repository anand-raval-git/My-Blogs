AWS offers six primary strategies for migrating applications to the cloud, each with its own approach and benefits:

1. **Rehosting (Lift-and-Shift)**: This method involves moving applications to the cloud without making any changes to them. It's a straightforward approach that allows companies to migrate quickly and scale their operations efficiently. For example, a company might move its existing virtual machines from an on-premises data center to Amazon EC2 instances in the cloud, maintaining the same configurations and settings.
    
2. **Replatforming**: Also known as "lift-tinker-and-shift," this strategy involves making a few optimizations to the application to take advantage of cloud capabilities, without altering its core architecture. For instance, a company might migrate its database to a managed service like Amazon RDS to reduce the overhead of database management while keeping the application largely unchanged.
    
3. **Refactoring**: This approach requires reimagining the application's architecture to leverage cloud-native features. It is typically driven by a strong business need to enhance features, improve scalability, or boost performance. For example, a company might refactor a monolithic application into a microservices architecture using AWS Lambda and other serverless technologies to achieve greater flexibility and scalability.
    
4. **Repurchasing**: This strategy involves switching from a traditional software license to a software-as-a-service (SaaS) model. A common example is migrating from an on-premises customer relationship management (CRM) system to a cloud-based solution like [Salesforce.com](http://Salesforce.com), which can offer more features and easier maintenance.
    
5. **Retaining**: In some cases, businesses may choose to keep certain applications in their current environment, especially if they are critical to operations or require significant refactoring before migration. This strategy allows companies to defer the migration of complex applications until they are ready.
    
6. **Retiring**: During the migration process, companies may identify components of their infrastructure that are no longer necessary. These components can be retired, meaning they are not moved to the cloud, which can help reduce costs and simplify the overall IT landscape. For example, outdated applications that are no longer used can be decommissioned instead of being migrated.
